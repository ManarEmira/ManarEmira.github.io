SMART IRRIGATION SYSTEM

A modern and innovative approach to irrigation

About Project

A smart irrigation system is an innovative solution to manage plant irrigation and conserve water resources. In addition to the features mentioned earlier, some smart irrigation systems incorporate a solar panel to power the system, making it even more energy-efficient and environmentally friendly.

The system uses sensors to collect data on environmental conditions, including humidity, temperature, and rainfall. The collected data is analyzed by a microcontroller, which adjusts the irrigation system accordingly. The microcontroller also communicates with an LCD display, showing the plant's condition, water requirements, and soil moisture levels.

The microcontroller is responsible for controlling the irrigation system, which includes pumps that deliver water to the plants. The microcontroller analyzes the data collected by the sensors and uses this information to determine when and how much water to supply to the plants. This ensures that the plants receive the optimal amount of water they need to grow and stay healthy.


Design

Base is divided into to levels the first level is Infrastructure which is the basic structures and systems required for operation, it consists of water infrastructure and electrical Infrastructure, and the second level is the main ground base where straight and curved slots used in it to facilitate the wire management and the pipes entrance to the tanks.



Two switched are being used on each side a circular one that controls the operation of the overall project.

Electric Enclosure which is the main brain of the system, the electric system is responsible for controlling the power supply to the various components of the system, including the sensors, pumps, and LCD display. It ensures that the system operates efficiently and reliably.

The monitoring system consists of various sensors that monitor the soil moisture, temperature, and other environmental conditions in the plant's vicinity. These sensors collect data and transmit it to the control unit, which processes the information and makes decisions about when and how much water to deliver to the plants.

The water tanks serve as a reservoir for the water that is used to irrigate the plants. The system pumps water from the tanks to the plants as needed, based on the data collected by the sensors and processed by the control unit. This ensures that the plants receive the right amount of water without overwatering or wasting water.

The greenhouses made of acrylic serve as a protective enclosure for the plants, providing them with the right amount of sunlight and protection from the elements. The acrylic material used in the greenhouses allows sunlight to penetrate through, providing the plants with the necessary amount of light for photosynthesis. The greenhouses also help to regulate the temperature and humidity levels inside, creating an optimal growing environment for the plants.



Fabrication

MDF wood and acrylic that were cut using a laser cutting machine. MDF wood is a popular material in woodworking due to its strength and durability, while acrylic is a lightweight and versatile plastic material that is widely used in various applications.



the use of LEDs to indicate the water levels in the main tank and emergency tank. By integrating LEDs into the system, I can easily monitor the water levels and ensure that the plants receive the right amount of water. The LED indicators for the main and emergency tanks are designed to emit different colors to indicate the water level, with green indicating a full tank, and red indicating that the water level is low and needs to be refilled.



The smart irrigation system project involves the use of two water tanks and pipes that are extended from them to the soil in order to provide moisture. The system is designed to monitor the moisture level of the soil and automatically water the plants when needed, thus ensuring that they receive the optimal amount of water. The system is equipped with sensors that detect the moisture level of the soil and trigger the watering system when the level falls below a certain threshold. The watering system is controlled by a microcontroller, which regulates the flow of water from the tanks to the soil. This project is highly efficient and can help conserve water by providing the plants with only the amount of water they need.
