COLD PLATE FOR ELECTRICAL INVERTER IN AN ELECTRIC VEHICLE

Cold Plate Design, developed for McMaster University at Mcmaster Automotive Resource Centre (MARC)

About Project

The Cold Plate for an Electrical Inverter is a high-performance thermal management solution designed for electric vehicles, ensuring efficient heat dissipation from power electronics. This project focuses on optimizing cooling to enhance the reliability and efficiency of inverter components.

One of the key features of this cold plate design is its advanced fluid channel geometry, which maximizes heat transfer while minimizing pressure drop. The design integrates press-fit aluminum pin fins, enhancing convective heat dissipation. A water-glycol coolant mixture is used to regulate temperatures effectively under high power loads, ensuring stable operation.

In addition to its thermal efficiency, the cold plate employs EPDM gaskets and O-rings to provide a robust sealing mechanism, preventing leaks and maintaining performance under varying pressure conditions. Its lightweight yet durable construction contributes to the overall efficiency of the EV powertrain, making it a crucial component for next-generation SiC-based inverters.



Design






The cold plate plays a crucial role in the thermal management of an electrical inverter, efficiently dissipating heat from power electronics to ensure reliable operation. The design incorporates a network of fluid channels that optimize coolant flow, enhancing heat transfer while minimizing pressure drop. The cold plate is specifically engineered to accommodate high-power SiC modules, enabling efficient cooling under demanding conditions.



The exploded view of the cold plate highlights its key components, including the gasket, grooves, bolts, and the power module with its baseplate. The gasket ensures a secure seal to prevent coolant leakage, while the grooves provide precise alignment and sealing support. The bolts fasten the cold plate assembly, ensuring structural integrity under operational stresses. The power module, mounted on its baseplate, interfaces directly with the cold plate to facilitate efficient heat transfer, allowing the inverter to maintain optimal thermal performance in electric vehicle applications.

Thermal Simulations

The thermal performance of the cold plate was evaluated through simulation under high-power operating conditions, assuming heat losses of 100 watts per die. A 50% water-ethylene glycol (WEG) mixture was used as the coolant, circulated at a flow rate of 10 liters per minute (LPM). This setup enabled realistic modeling of heat dissipation and allowed for assessment of the cooling strategy’s effectiveness in maintaining safe operating temperatures. The simulation results showed a maximum fluid velocity of approximately 2 m/s, a peak temperature of 116.5 °C—which remains safely below the 150 °C maximum limit of the power modules—and a maximum pressure of 6030 Pa. These findings provided valuable insights into the system’s fluid dynamics, temperature distribution, and overall thermal performance.
