RC Car

A miniature vehicle that is powered by an electric motor and controlled remotely through a PS Controller.

About Project

The RC car project with a steering system controlled by the PS controller and designed to play Rocket League in a competition setting with a microcontroller and DC motors is an incredible example of the versatility and innovation that can be achieved with remote-controlled vehicles.

The use of a PS controller to control the steering system provides a more intuitive and precise control scheme that is familiar to many gamers. With the PS controller adapter, the buttons and joysticks on the controller can be mapped to the different functions of the RC car, allowing for a customized and comfortable control experience.

The use of a microcontroller and DC motors to control the car's movements in a game like Rocket League is an impressive feat. This setup provides a level of precision and responsiveness that traditional remote-controlled cars cannot match. The car's low center of gravity and responsive suspension system allow it to move quickly and accurately on the playing field.



Design

Designing an RC car to play Rocket League in a competition setting requires careful consideration of the game's mechanics and requirements. The car needs to be agile, responsive, and maneuverable to succeed in the game. The use of DC motors and a microcontroller allows for quick and accurate control of the car, giving it a competitive edge in any competition.



The top of the RC car has been designed with spoilers and LED lights to improve both its appearance and performance. The spoilers create downforce on the rear wheels, improving traction and stability in turns, while the LED lights provide visibility and add an extra level of customization to the car's design. Together, these features make the RC car stand out and give it a competitive edge in speed and agility.

The RC car has been designed with the batteries located on the bottom part of the car, making it convenient to recharge the batteries without removing them from the car. This feature eliminates the need for extra steps and simplifies the recharging process, ensuring that the car is always ready for use. Additionally, having the batteries located on the bottom of the car provides a lower center of gravity, which can help to improve the car's stability and handling on the playing field.



The two-level design of the RC car is a clever way to optimize the use of space and improve its overall performance. The first level is dedicated to the motors and batteries, which are positioned for maximum stability and power. This level also serves as a base for the car, providing a solid foundation that can withstand the rigors of intense gameplay.

The second level of the RC car is reserved for the microcontroller, which controls the car's movements and responses to user inputs. This level is strategically placed on top of the motors and batteries, allowing for easy access and visibility. Additionally, this design feature helps to prevent interference from the motors and batteries, which can cause glitches and malfunctions in the microcontroller.


RC Version 2

In response to the competition's requirements, a new version of the RC car was designed to include two cars that can function as a team. The two separate RC cars that are identical in shape and size, with each car having its own microcontroller and DC motor system.




Fabrication

By using MDF wood in the fabrication process, the RC car is both affordable and lightweight, making it easier to maneuver on the playing field. Additionally, the durability of MDF wood ensures that the car can withstand the wear and tear of intense gameplay without breaking or falling apart.



Winning the third place was a significant milestone in the team's journey, and it was a proud moment for everyone involved.
