COOLING SYSTEM FOR A THREE PHASE INVERTER

Cooling System Design, developed for McMaster University at Mcmaster Automotive Resource Centre (MARC)

About Project

This project focuses on the development of an advanced cooling system designed specifically for a three-phase inverter used in electric vehicles. Effective thermal management is critical to ensure the reliable performance and longevity of the inverter's power electronics, especially under high power and switching conditions.

The cooling system utilizes a liquid-cooled design that incorporates strategically placed jet impingement nozzles to target high heat flux regions directly on the power module baseplates. This setup ensures rapid heat removal from critical SiC-based components, enhancing thermal uniformity and overall efficiency.

A water-glycol coolant mixture circulates through the system, absorbing and transferring heat away from the modules. The housing includes precision-machined sealing grooves and EPDM gaskets to prevent coolant leakage under varying temperature and pressure conditions. This robust and compact cooling solution supports sustained inverter operation in demanding EV environments.



Design






The cooling system is essential for maintaining the thermal stability of a three-phase inverter, ensuring optimal performance of the power electronics during high-load conditions. By targeting heat-sensitive components such as SiC power modules, the system effectively removes excess heat and prevents thermal degradation. The design features a high-efficiency liquid cooling loop that promotes uniform temperature distribution and minimizes hotspots, thereby enhancing the overall reliability and efficiency of the inverter in electric vehicle applications.
