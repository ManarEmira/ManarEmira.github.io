SMART CITY PROTOTYPE

A technological concept that aims to use advanced technologies and data analysis to improve the quality of life for citizens

About Project

The concept of a smart city involves the use of advanced technologies to make urban environments more efficient, sustainable, and livable. One of the areas where this technology can be applied is in the management of parking. By using sensors and LCD displays at the entrance of a parking lot, the availability of parking slots can be monitored in real-time. When slots are available, the gate opens automatically, allowing the car to enter. This eliminates the need for drivers to search for available parking spaces, reducing traffic congestion and emissions in the process.

In addition to the smart parking system, a smart city can also incorporate a smart lighting system. Traditional traffic lights use timers to control their operation, resulting in wasted energy when there is no traffic on the roads. A smart lighting system, on the other hand, uses sensors such as Light Dependent Resistors (LDRs) to detect the presence of vehicles and adjust the traffic signals accordingly. This not only saves energy but also reduces traffic congestion by optimizing traffic flow and reducing wait times at intersections.

Design

In order to simulate a real-life city in a project, various design elements must be considered. Buildings, trees, traffic lights, parking slots, and roads are all important components of a city and must be included in the project design.



Firstly, buildings and trees can be added to the project design using 3D modeling software. The size, shape, and placement of each building and tree should be considered to accurately reflect a real-life city. Textures and lighting can also be added to create a more realistic and immersive environment.



Next, traffic lights can be added to the design using either 3D modeling software or by using pre-made models available online. The position of each traffic light should be determined based on their real-life placement in a typical city.



Parking slots can be added to the project using 3D modeling software as well. The size and placement of each parking slot should be determined based on the real-life parking standards of the city being simulated. Additionally, the smart parking system described earlier can be implemented into the project to enhance the realism and functionality of the simulation.
