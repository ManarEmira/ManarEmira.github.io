Busbar Designs

Busbar designs developed for an electric vehivle inverter and converter

About Project

The project includes the development of advanced bus bar designs specifically engineered for electric vehicle inverters and converters. These bus bars feature a low-inductance, laminated structure that minimizes electromagnetic interference (EMI) and enhances switching performance, which is essential for SiC-based power modules. By incorporating multi-layer configurations with integrated insulation, the designs ensure efficient current distribution and reduce thermal hotspots. Their compact and modular form factor supports high power density applications, while also simplifying assembly and improving system reliability. These innovations contribute to the overall electrical efficiency and durability of next-generation EV power conversion systems.



Drawings & Designs






The busbar design for the inverter is optimized for high-current, low-inductance operation, tailored specifically for the compact layout of EV power electronics. Fabricated from 2 mm thick oxygen-free copper, the busbars are laser-cut for precision and bent using a CNC process, with a minimum bending radius of 3 mm (1.5× thickness) to avoid mechanical stress and maintain structural integrity. The configuration adopts a laminated design, stacking positive and negative conductors with 0.2 mm polyimide insulation between layers to minimize loop inductance and suppress EMI—crucial for fast-switching SiC modules. Terminal pads are surface-finished with a tin plating to ensure corrosion resistance and solder compatibility, while mounting holes are aligned with the inverter’s power module to minimize path resistance and assembly complexity. The busbars also incorporate notches and cutouts at strategic locations to accommodate connector interfaces and thermal expansion, improving both mechanical robustness and electrical performance under dynamic EV operating conditions.



The CAD model highlights key components of the power electronics assembly, including the busbars, capacitor bank, and inverter. The busbars play a critical role in electrically connecting the capacitor bank to the inverter, ensuring a reliable and low-impedance path for current flow. Their layout is designed to minimize inductance and support efficient power transfer, which is essential for maintaining stable operation in electric vehicle applications. Proper integration of these components is vital for both electrical performance and thermal management.
